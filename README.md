# NYC Restaurant Food Safety Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes restaurant inspection data from New York City to uncover patterns in food safety compliance across boroughs, cuisines, and violation types. Using historical inspection records from 2015 to 2025, the analysis identifies areas with elevated food safety risks and highlights opportunities for more targeted public health interventions.

## Problem Statement

With thousands of restaurants operating across New York City, maintaining consistent food safety standards is a significant challenge. The large volume of inspection data makes it difficult to quickly identify recurring issues, high-risk locations, and emerging trends.

This project aims to transform raw inspection records into actionable insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

## Objectives

* Analyze inspection outcomes and violation trends over time.
* Identify the most common food safety violations.
* Compare food safety performance across boroughs.
* Examine risk patterns among different cuisine categories.
* Highlight areas that may require increased monitoring or support.

## Data Preparation

The dataset contained over 280,000 inspection records and required extensive cleaning before analysis.

Key cleaning steps included:

* Standardizing date formats.
* Removing duplicate inspection records.
* Handling missing and inconsistent values.
* Creating calculated fields for risk analysis.
* Categorizing violations based on severity levels.

## Tools Used

* Excel – Data cleaning and preparation
* SQL – Data querying and aggregation
* Power BI – Dashboard development and visualization

## Key Insights

### Violation Trends

Several violation codes appeared consistently across multiple years, indicating recurring operational and hygiene challenges within the restaurant industry.

### Borough Performance

Food safety outcomes varied across boroughs, with some areas showing higher average risk scores and lower inspection grades than others.

### Cuisine Analysis

Certain cuisine categories recorded higher frequencies of violations, suggesting the need for more targeted food safety education and compliance support.

### Long-Term Performance

Inspection results revealed noticeable shifts in compliance levels over the ten-year period, highlighting both improvements and areas requiring continued attention.

## Recommendations

* Prioritize inspections in consistently high-risk locations.
* Develop targeted training programs for frequently cited violations.
* Increase preventive education efforts for restaurant operators.
* Use historical inspection trends to support proactive enforcement strategies.

## Dashboard Features

* Borough Risk Analysis
* Violation Code Breakdown
* Cuisine Performance Comparison
* Inspection Grade Trends
* Year-over-Year Safety Analysis

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analytics can be used to support public health initiatives by transforming large inspection datasets into meaningful insights. The findings provide a foundation for more efficient resource allocation, targeted interventions, and improved food safety outcomes across New York City.
